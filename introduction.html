What Are Microplastics?

Microplastics are plastic particles less than 5 mm in size. They are found in water, air, and soil, infiltrating ecosystems and the food chain.

How Do They Form?

  • Primary Microplastics: Manufactured for industrial use (e.g., microbeads in cosmetics).
  • Secondary Microplastics: Formed by the breakdown of larger plastics.
